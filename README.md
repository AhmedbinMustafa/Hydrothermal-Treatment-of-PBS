## 💡 The Idea

We took biodegradable **PBS plastic pellets**, treated them with hot pressurised water, then used supercritical CO₂ to simultaneously expand them into **foam balls (~5 cm diameter)** and load them with **ibuprofen**.

> Instead of a pill that floods the body with medicine all at once — these foam balls sit at a target site and **slowly release ibuprofen over days or weeks** as the material naturally biodegrades. No surgery needed to remove them. No toxic residue left behind.

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

| Problem | Our Solution |
|---|---|
| Pills release drug all at once → spike then crash | Foam biodegrades slowly → steady, controlled release |
| Implants need removal surgery | PBS breaks down naturally in the body |
| Traditional drug loading uses toxic solvents | Supercritical CO₂ is non-toxic, leaves zero residue |
| Polystyrene foam pollutes the environment | PBS is 100% biodegradable in soil and water |

**Target applications:** post-surgical pain relief · wound dressing · tissue engineering scaffolds · orthopaedic implants

## ⚗️ What We Did

### Step 1 — Sample Preparation & Weighing

<div align="center">

<img width="400" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2c25b732-3b26-4662-9ba9-bbbb910c64ed" alt="Mesh sachet for PBS pellets"/>

*▲ Metal mesh sachet used to pack and contain PBS pellets during hydrothermal treatment*

</div>

Each PBS sample was weighed to approximately **30 mg** on an analytical balance, packed tightly into a mesh sachet, and labelled with its condition number and mass. Around **15 sachets** were loaded per run into a metal mesh basket.

---

### Step 2 — Hydrothermal Pre-treatment (HT)

<div align="center">

https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/024a552a-a9f8-4183-87bf-d6680de9bc4e

*▲ Sealing the batch reactor before hydrothermal treatment*

</div>

The basket was placed inside a sealed batch reactor with **750 mL of Milli-Q water**, purged with nitrogen, and heated. The hot pressurised water conditions the PBS at a molecular level — softening its crystalline structure and increasing porosity so the CO₂ and ibuprofen can penetrate uniformly in the next step.

<div align="center">

| Parameter | Value |
|:---|:---|
| Water volume | 750 mL |
| Peak temperature | 157 °C |
| Peak pressure | 29.6 bar |
| Heating rate | ~6.4 °C/min |
| Initial N₂ pressure | ~18.2 bar |
| Cooling method | Natural + ice-water bath |

</div>

---

### Step 3 — Supercritical CO₂ Foaming & Ibuprofen Impregnation

<div align="center">

<img width="400" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fbeb9d83-4c61-4526-870f-f13e8be4f48c" alt="PBS foam ball after CO2 foaming"/>

*▲ PBS foam ball immediately after supercritical CO₂ foaming — still in its aluminium foil support*

</div>

Pre-treated samples were placed in a high-pressure vessel with ibuprofen and pressurised with CO₂ using a **Thar SFC high-pressure pump**. Under supercritical conditions, CO₂ acts as a solvent — it dissolves the ibuprofen and drives it deep into the PBS matrix while expanding the material into a foam. Rapid depressurisation over **2–3 minutes** causes the CO₂ to escape, leaving the ibuprofen permanently trapped inside the foam structure.



---

### Step 4 — Characterisation & Storage

After each stage (HT and foaming), samples were measured for **mass** (analytical balance) and **volume** (calliper). One sample per condition was analysed visually using **Fotocol**. All samples stored in labelled bags with date and experimental conditions.
